Portugal banking at a glance

Portugal has a mature retail banking market with a mix of large domestic banks, international groups and digital-first options. For most newcomers, the practical decision is not only which bank is "best", but which bank will accept your current documentation, how easy the onboarding path is, and what you will pay every month for basic banking.

Resident vs non-residentEligibility and onboarding rules vary sharply by bank and by channel.
Digital onboardingOften faster, but usually more restrictive than branch-based opening.
Main comparison pointMonthly fee structure, app quality, branch support and document fit.

Documents banks commonly ask for in Portugal

In practice, Portuguese banks usually want a core file that proves identity, tax registration, address and economic activity. The exact mix changes by bank, nationality, account type and whether you apply online or in branch, but preparing these documents in advance materially reduces rejection risk.

Core documents almost everyone should have ready

1) Valid identification

Passport, EU identity card, Portuguese Citizen Card or residence card/title. Some banks ask for the original document at the appointment or during a video-identification step.

2) Portuguese tax number (NIF)

A document showing your Portuguese tax number. In many cases the NIF is mandatory for a standard current account.

3) Proof of address

Usually a recent utility bill, tax statement, official letter, bank statement or other address evidence. Several banks apply recency limits, so use a recent document where possible.

4) Proof of professional status or source of funds

This is where profiles start to diverge. Banks often want a document showing whether you are employed, self-employed, retired, a student, unemployed or a company owner.

Important: if you are a foreign national, some banks may also ask for residency evidence, residence permit documentation, or additional source-of-funds clarification. Branch opening is usually more flexible than fully digital onboarding for non-standard profiles.

Documents by profile

Employee / salaried worker

  • Recent payslip
  • Employment contract, or employer declaration
  • In some cases, proof of employer name / professional entity

This is the most standard profile. A recent salary slip plus contract or employer letter is usually the cleanest file.

Freelancer / independent worker

  • Recibos verdes or equivalent invoicing proof
  • Statement of start of activity from the tax authority
  • Client declaration, where relevant
  • Professional association card, if your activity depends on one

Freelancers should be ready to prove both activity and income pattern. This profile is often checked more closely than standard salaried employment.

Self-employed company owner / shareholder-manager

  • Commercial Registry certificate or company registration extract
  • Proof of ownership or management role
  • Latest remuneration proof, dividend evidence or company income support where available

If your income comes through a company, bring documents that connect you personally to that company and explain the source of funds clearly.

Retired / pensioner

  • Pension statement or pension payment slip
  • Bank statement showing pension credits, if needed
  • Tax or social security document supporting pension status

Retired applicants should prioritise a clear pension proof plus regular address and identification documents.

Student

  • Proof of enrolment or student declaration
  • Identification and NIF
  • Address proof
  • In some cases, proof of funds or sponsor support

Student products can have their own rules. For foreign students, banks may also ask for residence-related documents depending on status and nationality.

Unemployed / not currently working

  • Unemployment statement or employment-centre registration, where applicable
  • Alternative economic situation proof if not registered
  • Source-of-funds support for expected account use

When there is no current salary or business activity, banks may pay more attention to how the account will be funded.

Useful preparation before you go to the bank

  1. Make sure your name is written the same way across passport, NIF and address proof.
  2. Bring originals when possible, not only screenshots.
  3. Use recent documents, especially for address and employment status.
  4. If your documents are foreign, keep translations or supporting explanations ready if the bank asks.
  5. If your case is not straightforward, book an in-branch appointment instead of relying on the fastest online flow.
Practical note: no single checklist works for every bank. Millennium bcp, Santander and ActivoBank explicitly ask for proof of address plus proof of profession or status, and official examples include payslips, contracts, recibos verdes, start-of-activity statements, pension-related evidence and unemployment support depending on profile. CGD's online channel is more restrictive and is geared to applicants with Portuguese citizen credentials and Portuguese tax residence.

How account opening usually works in 2026

  1. Get your NIF and make sure your name and address are consistent across documents.
  2. Decide whether you qualify for online opening or need an in-branch appointment.
  3. Check fee sheets, card conditions and whether initial funding is required.
  4. Prepare proof of profession, income or source of funds in advance.
  5. After opening, test app access, card activation, SEPA transfer capability and MB WAY or other daily payment tools.
Best path for straightforward resident cases: banks with mature online onboarding can be materially faster.
Best path for non-standard cases: branch appointments are often better for non-residents, foreign passports, mixed tax residence situations or incomplete residence documentation.
Best path for fee control: compare package accounts against basic needs; some users overpay for bundled accounts they do not actually need.

Latest Portugal banking news and sector signals

If you are choosing a bank now, current sector conditions matter. Profitability, deposit trends, supervision priorities and digital banking changes all affect how banks price products and how aggressively they onboard customers.

That points to cleaner asset quality than in earlier years, which supports a more stable operating environment across the sector.

Quick answers

  • Can a non-resident open a bank account?
    Yes, in some cases, but requirements vary by bank and by branch. Online channels are usually more restrictive.

  • What is the most common blocker?
    Mismatch between identity, NIF, address and source-of-funds documents.

  • Which bank is cheapest?
    There is no universal cheapest option. Compare maintenance fees, card fees and package conditions before opening.
