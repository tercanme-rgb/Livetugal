The fast take: your visa category is a money question, not a mood question

TL;DR: if your income lands because you work online, you probably belong in the D8 conversation. If your income lands because you have recurring passive or own-income streams such as pensions, dividends, royalties, or rent, you probably begin with D7.

Portugal still sells the dream well. The admin system does not care about the dream. It cares whether your file is coherent, boring, and easy to approve.

The cleanest applicants do not always have the most money. They have the cleanest story, the best sequencing, and the least contradiction inside the file.

D7 vs D8: the distinction people blur, and then pay for

D7: the recurring-income lane

Think pensions, rental income, royalties, dividends, and other income that does not require you to actively work this month for cash to appear.

  • stronger for passive or recurring own-income logic
  • useful for retirees and applicants with stable independent income
  • works best when the income story is calm, documented, and repetitive

D8: the remote-work lane

Think remote employees, consultants, contractors, freelancers, and founders with provable personal remote income tied to work performed outside Portugal.

  • stronger when income comes from active remote labor
  • good fit for digital nomads who want an actual residence path
  • depends heavily on proving the income is real, stable, and external
The simple rule: if the money arrives because you worked online, start with D8. If the money arrives whether you worked this month or not, look hard at D7.

AIMA is where the move becomes real

A visa gets you in. It does not magically complete the residence story for you.

The residence-side administrative reality continues after arrival. That means your setup work should anticipate the next step, not just the consular step.

Before arrival

Choose the right route. Prepare the income logic. Build the document stack. Lock in credible accommodation.

At visa stage

Make the file coherent. Remove contradictions. Keep every page aligned to one story.

After landing

Stabilize address, banking, records, and move through the residency layer with less chaos.

Signal section

The 3-step playbook for people who want less drama

  1. Home prep: decide D7 or D8, obtain NIF support if needed, prepare banking logic, collect income proof, organize translations and core documents.
  2. Visa file: remove inconsistencies, tighten the housing story, and ensure every document supports the same narrative.
  3. Landing + residency: arrive ready to stabilize address, payments, paperwork and the residence-side process instead of improvising everything under time pressure.
